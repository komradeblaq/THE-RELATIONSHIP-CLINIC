HOW TO TRAIN CHILDREN WHO CAN SURVIVE WITHOUT YOU

Raising Independent, Responsible, and God-Fearing Children. Parenting is one of the greatest responsibilities God entrusts to humanity. Children are not given to parents to control forever, but to train, guide, and release into life as mature and responsible individuals. The goal of parenting is not to create lifelong dependence, but to nurture children who can stand strong in character, wisdom, and faith—even when their parents are no longer present.

The Bible clearly emphasises the importance of intentional training in raising children:
“Train up a child in the way he should go, and when he is old he will not depart from it.” — Proverbs 22:6

Training requires patience, wisdom, and deliberate effort. The following principles provide a practical and faith-based approach to raising children who are capable of navigating life successfully.

Chapter 1: Stop Doing Everything for Them

One of the greatest mistakes many parents make is doing everything for their children. In many homes, parents carry every responsibility while the child simply enjoys the benefits.

  • Parents wash the clothes.
  • Parents cook the meals.
  • Parents pack school bags.
  • Parents remind them about homework.
  • Parents solve every problem.

Conclusion: The True Success of Parenting

Every child will eventually step into the world alone. They will attend university, pursue careers, travel to new places, form families, and make life-changing decisions without their parents standing beside them. At that moment, parenting reveals its true results. Not the shouting. Not the control. But the training.

Successful parenting produces children who are responsible, resilient, wise, and grounded in faith. The greatest gift parents can give their children is not wealth or comfort. It is the ability to stand alone, make wise choices, and live with integrity. And when parents raise children who can thrive without them, they have fulfilled one of life’s greatest callings.
