HOW TO TRAIN CHILDREN WHO CAN SURVIVE WITHOUT YOU

Raising Independent, Responsible, and God-Fearing Children. Parenting is one of the greatest responsibilities God entrusts to humanity. Children are not given to parents to control forever, but to train, guide, and release into life as mature and responsible individuals. The goal of parenting is not to create lifelong dependence, but to nurture children who can stand strong in character, wisdom, and faith—even when their parents are no longer present.

The Bible clearly emphasises the importance of intentional training in raising children:
“Train up a child in the way he should go, and when he is old he will not depart from it.” — Proverbs 22:6

Training requires patience, wisdom, and deliberate effort. The following principles provide a practical and faith-based approach to raising children who are capable of navigating life successfully.

Chapter 1: Stop Doing Everything for Them

One of the greatest mistakes many parents make is doing everything for their children. In many homes, parents carry every responsibility while the child simply enjoys the benefits.

  • Parents wash the clothes.
  • Parents cook the meals.
  • Parents pack school bags.
  • Parents remind them about homework.
  • Parents solve every problem.

Meanwhile, the child grows older without learning how to manage basic responsibilities. By adolescence, a child should already be developing independence. If a teenager cannot wash clothes, prepare simple meals, organise their belongings, or manage daily routines, then an important aspect of their development has been neglected.

Children learn responsibility by doing, not by watching. The Bible teaches the importance of diligence and responsibility:
“The soul of the diligent shall be made rich.” — Proverbs 13:4

Chapter 2: Teach Decision-Making Early

Many parents unknowingly raise dependent children by controlling every decision in their lives. They choose what the child wears, friends they keep, what to study, and nearly every aspect of life. Decision-making is a skill that develops through practice. Children should be given opportunities to make small choices early in life, learning from mistakes. The Scriptures emphasise wisdom:
“If any of you lacks wisdom, let him ask of God, who gives to all liberally.” — James 1:5

Chapter 3: Teach Financial Responsibility

Financial ignorance has caused many young adults to struggle in life. Children should learn early about saving, budgeting, and responsible spending. Start with small allowances and guide them on prioritising needs over wants. The Bible encourages wisdom in managing resources:
“The wise store up choice food and olive oil, but fools gulp theirs down.” — Proverbs 21:20

Chapter 4: Build Emotional Strength

Life is full of challenges. Children shielded from discomfort may struggle with real-life problems. Parents must help them develop resilience, teaching how to handle disappointment, recover from failure, apologise when wrong, and move forward with courage. The Bible reminds us:
“Let perseverance finish its work so that you may be mature and complete, lacking nothing.” — James 1:4

Chapter 5: Stop Making Them Afraid of You

Fear produces temporary obedience but rarely confidence or maturity. Healthy parenting requires both authority and approachability. Children should respect but also feel safe to communicate openly. The Bible teaches:
“Fathers, do not provoke your children to anger, but bring them up in the training and instruction of the Lord.” — Ephesians 6:4

Chapter 6: Teach Practical Life Skills

By the teenage years, children should possess basic life skills: cooking, laundry, cleaning, communication, asking for help, and personal safety. Home is the first classroom. The Bible affirms:
“Prepare your work outside; get everything ready for yourself in the field.” — Proverbs 24:27

Chapter 7: Teach Faith and Values

Beyond skills, children need a strong moral and spiritual foundation: integrity, self-control, honesty, and reverence for God. Faith includes prayer, moral discipline, and righteous living. The Scriptures highlight:
“These commandments that I give you today are to be on your hearts. Impress them on your children.” — Deuteronomy 6:6–7

Conclusion: The True Success of Parenting

Every child will eventually step into the world alone: university, careers, new places, families, and life-changing decisions. Parenting reveals its true results not in shouting or control, but in training. Successful parenting produces children who are responsible, resilient, wise, and grounded in faith. The greatest gift is the ability to stand alone, make wise choices, and live with integrity.
